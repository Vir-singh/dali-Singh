Kamata Nath

Kamadgiri, also known as the heart of Chitrakoot or the original Chitrakoot, is a hill believed to have been home to Goddess Sita, Lord Rama, and Lord Lakshmana during their exile.

Hanuman Dhara

This place is called Hanuman Dhara because of a gentle stream which flows over the statue of Lord Hanuman. To the devotees, this is perhaps the most beautiful sight to come across

Sabari Waterfall

Shabari waterfall (Shabari Jal Prapat) has three water bodies that fall from 40 feet parallelly, followed by a 60-foot-wide water bed (pool) and one more waterfall that leads to a larger water body that disappears in the forest.

Ram Ghat

Ram Ghat is a popular and sacred destination located in the state of Uttar Pradesh. It is situated on the banks of the Mandakini River.

Bharatkoop

Bharat Koop is a large well and sacred place of worship in Chitrakoot, Uttar Pradesh, India that is believed to contain water from all the holy rivers. It is located near Bharatpur village
